# Terminology

The following terms are self-defined and used exclusively within the conceptual context of NeusktchmechArc. Any resemblance to existing concepts, metaphors, or creative practices is unintended and coincidental. Title Case is applied consistently to defined conceptual components throughout this archive.

---

### Interior Atlas

The internal expanse of experience: a vast and evolving expanse of internal impressions, recollections, and imagined states that forms the originating domain of NeusktchmechArc. Conceived as a form of experiential geography, composed of layered terrains of deferred inclination, structured regions of memory, and quiet reservoirs of positive valence. It serves as the source domain from which all creative impulses within this framework originate.

### First Emanation

The initial outward emergence of an internal impression, the moment an internal state begins to take provisional form beyond the interior domain — often a sketch, a brief written fragment, or an initial configuration of colour. This marks the conceptual emergence of an artwork and often occurs without deliberate planning.

### Tranquil Rift

A deliberate pause within the creative progression, an interval of reduced activity that forms a transitional space between initial emergence and subsequent development. It functions as a stabilising interval in which preliminary material is allowed to settle, regarded within NeusktchmechArc as a protected interval of attention.

### Integration Phase

The stage at which the artwork advances from a provisional state into a resolved and cohesive configuration, through the deliberate consolidation of meaning into form. At this stage the artwork becomes layered with a personal internal code that functions as its underlying structural logic.

### Internal Coherence

The internal consistency through which relationships between locations and visual references are apprehended within the Interior Atlas, giving NeusktchmechArc its defining quality. Internal coherence functions as the only point of completion for a given work.

### Conceptual Provenance

The function of this document and of the NeusktchmechArc framework more broadly: recording the conditions from which each artwork emerges, and documenting how and why the work takes form rather than retroactively assigning meaning.

### Framework

Used solely as a narrative and reflective descriptor throughout this archive. It does not represent or imply a system, methodology, model, process, guideline, or functional structure of any kind.

### Archive

Used in a descriptive sense to indicate continuity of reference, not institutional storage or cataloguing.
